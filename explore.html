History

In addition to its vast size, the Mariana Trench is also one Earth's oldest formations.


The Mariana Trench

Deepest an Oldest Trench in the world

The Mariana's seafloor has been estimated to be as old as 180 million years! Just as many deepwater ocean trenches, is formed by the geologic process of subduction. Subduction is a process that takes place at convergent boundaries of tectonic plates where one plate moves under another, sinking into the mantle due to gravity. In the case of oceanic subduction, a plate topped by oceanic crust is subducted beneath another plate topped by oceanic crust.

Another contributing factor to the trench's depth is the natural process of under water volcanoes "venting out". Seafloor is known to be formed as lava spreads at mid-ocean ridges. Fresh lava rides high on the underlying mantle due to its comparitive warm temperature and rekative buoyancy. As lava ages and spreads away from its origin, it becomes much denser as it cools and solidifies. This causes it to settle even lower than previously spawned and the Mariana Trench is a clear example of this consecutive process.
